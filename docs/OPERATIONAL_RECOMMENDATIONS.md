# Operational Recommendations

AI1SAD recommendations describe surveillance and safety-review patterns. They are not commands, safety guarantees, or attack predictions.

## Recommendation Keys

### `reef_edge_expanding_grid`

Use for reef, dropoff, spearfishing, diving, and reef-edge activity contexts.

Recommended action: prioritize reef edge, dropoff, and adjacent current lines with expanding drone passes.

### `inlet_surf_zone_scan`

Use for inlet, river-mouth, runoff, and surf-zone contexts.

Recommended action: scan inlet outflow, surf zone, and adjacent sandbars with repeated parallel passes.

### `shoreline_parallel_sweep`

Use for general shoreline operations where human exposure or moderate surveillance context is active.

Recommended action: run parallel shoreline sweeps and maintain lookout coverage where human exposure is highest.

### `post_sighting_focus_area`

Use when recent verified sighting or sighting-cluster signals are active.

Recommended action: concentrate monitoring around the latest verified sighting while checking adjacent travel corridors.

### `carcass_event_buffer_zone`

Use for carcass, fish-kill, or high-impact biological event contexts.

Recommended action: create a temporary buffer around the biological event and coordinate with wildlife authorities.

### `low_priority_observation`

Use when scores and available signals support routine observation.

Recommended action: maintain routine observation and defer intensive drone coverage unless new signals arrive.

## Interpretation

Recommendations should be read with local lifeguard, emergency, wildlife, and weather guidance. AI1SAD can prioritize where to look first; it does not determine whether water access is safe.
